About Me

Hi, my name is Ty Chen, a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo.
I have a passion for 3D modeling, whether it be in Blender or SolidWorks.
I enjoy turning ideas into tangible results!

I'm pursuing a degree in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Waterloo. Most recently, I completed my winter co-op term as an Engineering & Validation Lab Intern at Bend All Automotive ULC, where I conducted tensile and burst testing on automotive fluid lines, collaborated with engineers to refine testing methods, and applied APQP and DFMEA methodologies to improve validation processes. Previously, I completed a high school co-op term at Greentec and a full-time summer position there involving sustainable technology recycling. These roles have helped me build a solid foundation in design, manufacturing, problem-solving abilities, and technical communication.

Outside academics, I like to develop personal projects in Blender, exploring everything from isometric interiors to stylized weapons and low-poly environments. I have recently learned how to code and sometimes participate in coding challenges whenever possible! I have enjoyed practicing martial arts for the last ten years. In 2023, I earned an International First Degree Black Belt in Meibukan Goju-Ryu Karate and recently attained a Second Degree Black Belt. I love going to the gym and rock climbing, mainly bouldering (currently at V5). I spend my free time making music in FL Studio, allowing my creativity to flow!
